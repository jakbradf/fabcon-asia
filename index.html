The world's largest Microsoft Fabric and SQL conference, coming to Asia-Pacific

What is FABCON & SQLCON APAC?

FABCON started in the US and has grown into one of the largest community-run Microsoft data conferences in the world. FABCON & SQLCON APAC brings that to the region — a conference built for and by the people who actually work with Fabric and SQL. Not a vendor expo, not a product launch. A place where data engineers, analysts, BI developers, and SQL professionals share what's working in the real world.

Sydney is where it starts. The first APAC edition runs at ICC Sydney in April 2027, before the event moves to a new city across the region each year. FABCON covers Microsoft Fabric — data engineering, analytics, BI, governance, and AI. SQLCON runs alongside it, dedicated to SQL Server, Azure SQL, and the broader data platform. One ticket covers both. Microsoft is on board as a sponsor and will be sending product and engineering leads from both teams to take the stage.

Why FABCON & SQLCON APAC?

Whether you work in Fabric, SQL, or both — this is where the Asia-Pacific data community comes together to learn from people doing the actual work.

Keynotes from Microsoft

The product and engineering teams behind Fabric and SQL will be on stage in Sydney — not a recorded session, but real people talking about what’s shipping, what’s changing, and what’s on the roadmap.

Hands-on workshops

Full-day workshops across both Fabric and SQL tracks. Bring your laptop, come with actual questions, and leave with things you can put straight into practice back at work.

Sessions across both conferences

Five days of content across FABCON and SQLCON tracks — data engineering, analytics, Power BI, governance, AI, SQL Server, and Azure SQL. All led by practitioners, not vendor reps.

The APAC data community

Sydney is the first stop — FABCON & SQLCON APAC moves to a new city across the region each year. The people you meet here tend to become the colleagues you actually keep in touch with.

Access to the product teams

With Microsoft sponsoring both conferences, you get genuine access to PMs and engineers from the Fabric and SQL teams — people who can actually answer your questions about what's coming.

Real-world case studies

Hear how organisations across Asia-Pacific are running Fabric and SQL in production — what worked, what didn't, and what they'd do differently. The honest version, not the highlight reel.
